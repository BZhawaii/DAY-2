Team D-A-Y

Lauren Daley

Lauren is an architect licensed in both Colorado and California. She earned her undergraduate degree in architecture from the University of Florida and her Masters degree in architecture from the University of California, Berkeley. After graduate school, Lauren stayed in the Bay Area where she worked at multiple architecture firms specializing in various types of design. Lauren’s rich architectural experience includes: multi-family housing, student housing, affordable housing, senior housing, single-family residential, restaurant, straw bale, and environmentally progressive design.
While in California, Lauren worked as a community leader serving as a Hayes Valley Neighborhood Association Board Member. She also volunteered for the San Francisco Bicycle Coalition and at various community gardens throughout the Bay Area.
After ten wonderful years in the Bay Area it was time for a move to be close to family. Following her move to Loveland, Colorado, she founded D-A- Y Architects with her spouse and sister-in-law in 2017.
In her off hours, Lauren enjoys making lamps, playing her guitar, riding her bike, cooking, and spending time with her family.

Jessie Allen-Young

Jessie is an architect licensed in Colorado and California. She earned her Bachelors in Arts from Dartmouth College and her Masters in Architecture from the University of Michigan. Jessie has worked on a variety of projects in the San Francisco Bay Area as well as in northern Colorado from high-end residential houses, commercial projects, and schools.
Jessie served as the editor of the Hayes Valley Neighborhood Associations’ newsletter and website. Jessie also served as a board member of HVNA.
During summers growing up, Jessie spent her time working under her father’s guidance on residential construction jobsites learning wood framing, roofing, siding and general contracting.
Jessie enjoys running, working on projects around the house and being outdoors with her family.

Holly Allen-Young

Holly is a jack-of- all-trades. She graduated from Scripps College where she majored in Economics, Environment and Politics. She has been a project manager for geotechnical companies as well as tech companies. She has an entrepreneurial spirit and enjoys doing event planning as a side job.
Holly’s most recent job has being returning to work as a framer and general contractor for Two Dots Inc.
